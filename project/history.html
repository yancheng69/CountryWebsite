The History of China

China boasts one of the world's oldest continuous civilizations, with a recorded history spanning over 5,000 years. This long timeline, marked by dynastic cycles and immense periods of imperial rule, has profoundly shaped the country's unique philosophy, society, and art.

Ancient Foundations

The Qin and Han Dynasties (221 BCE - 220 CE): The unification under Qin Shi Huang and the subsequent establishment of the Silk Road during the Han era.

The Golden Age

The Tang and Song Dynasties (618 - 1279): Periods of immense cultural, economic, and technological flourishing, including inventions like gunpowder and the compass.

Modern Transformation

Late Imperial to Present Day: The decline of the Qing dynasty, the era of conflicts, and the founding of the People's Republic of China in 1949, leading to rapid modernization.
