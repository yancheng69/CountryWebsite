Chinese Culture

Festivals

The most important festival is Lunar New Year, when families gather for meals, give red envelopes with money, and set off fireworks. Another popular one is Mid-Autumn Festival, where people eat mooncakes and admire the full moon. more

Arts

Chinese calligraphy is more than writing—it's an art. Using a brush and ink, artists create characters that show emotion and skill. Paper cutting, often red, is used to decorate homes during festivals, with designs like flowers or animals. more

Philosophy

Confucianism teaches respect for elders and harmony in society. Taoism focuses on living in balance with nature, like the idea of "yin and yang"—opposites that work together. more
